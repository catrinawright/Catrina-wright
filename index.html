Professional Background

Not a Career Change.
A Career Convergence.

My path to special education is not a career change. It is a career convergence. Every role I held before the classroom — Research Assistant at the University of Chicago, Probation Officer at Cook County, Criminal Justice and Psychology scholar at Chicago State University — was building the same core competency: reading complex systems, advocating for vulnerable people inside those systems, and making evidence-based decisions under pressure. That is precisely what LBS1 special education requires.

My research background instilled the analytical rigor that now drives my instructional practice — progress monitoring, data-driven IEP goal-setting, and systematic instruction design built on the same discipline as empirical inquiry. My psychology minor provided the behavioral and developmental foundation that informs how I read student behavior, design interventions, and apply FBA frameworks in real time. My time inside the Cook County criminal justice system — where documentation errors carry real consequences and institutional compliance and human dignity are in constant tension — prepared me for the legal and ethical weight of special education in ways that traditional teacher preparation programs cannot replicate.

Today, I am completing a Master of Arts in Teaching with an LBS1 endorsement through the Chicago Public Schools Teacher Residency Program at National Louis University. My classroom at Ray Graham Training Center High School is a vocational simulation lab for transition-age students (ages 18-22) with intellectual and developmental disabilities. I arrived at this work equipped — not just credentialed.

IEP Jargon Tool

I built this fully coded, mobile-responsive digital dashboard to replace the static, overwhelming paper packets that routinely alienate families from the IEP process. It features six color-coded branches: IDEA Basics, the Endrew F. Standard, SEL and Safety, a dynamic searchable Traffic Light Glossary (Red = Rights · Yellow = Process · Green = Instruction), Advocacy Action Loops, and verifiable legal sources. I designed it in plain language with high-contrast visuals and intuitive navigation — accessible on any phone or computer via a single link. This is not a handout. It is an application.
